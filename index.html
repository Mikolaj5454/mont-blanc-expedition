The Expedition

Rising 4,808 meters above sea level, Mont Blanc is the undisputed monarch of the Alps and Western Europe. Our 7-8 day expedition is designed to maximize your chances of summiting through careful acclimatization, technical training, and world-class routing.

Departing from the historic mountaineering capital of Chamonix, France, we traverse pristine glaciers, navigate sheer rock faces, and sleep in high-altitude mountain refuges. This isn't just a hike; it's a true alpinism endeavor demanding respect, endurance, and spirit.

Expedition Itinerary

DAY 1

Arrival in Chamonix (1035m)

Transfer from Geneva Airport. Check-in at expedition hotel. Evening briefing with guides, gear check, and team introduction dinner.

DAY 2

Acclimatization Hike: Mer de Glace (1913m)

Morning hike to the famous Mer de Glace glacier. Introduction to crampon and ice axe technique on the glacier. Afternoon rest and preparation.

DAY 3

Training Day: Aiguille du Midi (3842m)

Cable car ascent to Aiguille du Midi. Technical training on snow and ice — self-arrest, rope teamwork, crevasse rescue basics. Return to Chamonix for overnight.

DAY 4

Ascent to Tête Rousse Hut (3167m)

Early morning departure. Hike through the Bionnassay glacier approach. Arrive at Tête Rousse refuge for lunch and overnight stay. Acclimatization walk in the afternoon.

DAY 5

Tête Rousse to Goûter Hut (3835m)

Cross the famous Grand Couloir — the most dangerous section of the normal route. Technical ridge climb to Refuge du Goûter. Early dinner and sleep by 6PM for summit push.

DAY 6

SUMMIT DAY: Mont Blanc (4808m)

Wake up at 1AM. Departure at 2AM under headlamps. Ascent via Vallot Emergency Shelter (4362m), Bosses Ridge to the summit. Summit arrival target: 7-9AM. Descend back to Goûter Hut, then all the way to Chamonix. Celebration dinner.

DAY 7

Reserve / Rest Day

Buffer day in case of bad weather on Day 6. Rest, optional easy hike, or repeat summit attempt depending on conditions.

DAY 8

Departure

Morning checkout. Transfer back to Geneva Airport. End of expedition.

Risks & Safety Protocol

The Mountain Commands Respect

An expedition to 4,808m is never without danger. We believe in absolute transparency regarding the hazards you will face.

Altitude Sickness (AMS) & Exhaustion

Above 3,000m, oxygen levels drop significantly. Physical exhaustion sets in rapidly. We mitigate this through an aggressive 3-day acclimatization protocol before the final ascent.

Sudden Weather Changes

Whiteouts and sub-zero blizzards can occur within minutes. We maintain 24/7 direct satellite links with Chamonix meteorological stations.

Crevasses & Avalanches

The glacier is a living entity. You will be roped to your IFMGA guide at all times while crossing objective hazard zones. All guides carry advanced crevasse rescue hardware.

Why Submit to the Suffering?

Because there is a moment, long before the world below has woken, when you stand upon the crest of the Bosses Ridge. The air is so thin it burns, your muscles scream in exhaustion, and the immense void falls away on either side. And then, the sun breaks the horizon. A sea of clouds stretches across France, Italy, and Switzerland. You look out and see the dark pyramid of the Matterhorn piercing the dawn. In that exact second, standing on the absolute roof of Europe, the pain vanishes. You are transformed. The memory of 4,808 meters will burn brightly in your soul for the rest of your life.
